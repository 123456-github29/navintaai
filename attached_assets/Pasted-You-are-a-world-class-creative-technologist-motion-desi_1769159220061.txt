Core direction (very important)

Navinta is an AI-powered director, not a tool.

Everything on the page should feel like:
	•	calm confidence
	•	motion with purpose
	•	subtle drama
	•	cinematic pacing
	•	intentional emptiness (negative space is allowed)
	•	no vibe-coded gradients
	•	no random colors
	•	no childish motion
	•	no over-decoration

Think: Apple + Opus Clip + Linear + Stripe + GSAP showcase
But adapted to video creation + directing

⸻

1. Hero Section (make it feel alive)

Keep the existing hero structure, but:

Add:
	•	A clapperboard animation that subtly enters at page load
	•	It can rotate once slowly
	•	Then rest near the logo or headline
	•	When scrolling, it should slightly parallax or tilt
	•	Animate the main headline text:
	•	Letters slightly separate on load
	•	Then gently settle into place
	•	On scroll, text morphs into:
“AI-Powered Directing”
	•	Add subtle film-grain overlay (very faint)
	•	Add slow ambient motion (background particles or light noise, NOT obvious)

Motion rules:
	•	Everything eases
	•	No bounce
	•	No spring
	•	No flashy effects
	•	Smooth GSAP timelines only
	•	Everything should feel like a film opening

⸻

2. Scroll Experience (VERY important)

As the user scrolls:
	•	Each section should feel like a scene change
	•	Use:
	•	fade + slide
	•	slight scale-in
	•	directional motion
	•	No section should appear instantly
	•	Use GSAP ScrollTrigger for everything

⸻

3. “How It Works” Section (make it cinematic)

Replace static cards with animated sequence blocks:

Step 1: Plan
	•	Text slides in from left
	•	Icons fade up from blur
	•	Subtle background motion

Step 2: Record
	•	Phone frame animates in
	•	Recording ring pulses once
	•	Camera UI subtly animates

Step 3: Edit
	•	Clips visually “snap together”
	•	Captions slide in line by line
	•	B-roll flashes for 1–2 seconds

Step 4: Publish
	•	Video frame zooms slightly
	•	Export animation
	•	Calm confirmation motion

Each step should feel like a short scene, not a box.

⸻

4. Add “Human Proof” (this is why site feels empty)

Add a section called:
“Made by real people, not editors”
	•	Use Pexels stock video
	•	Show people recording themselves
	•	Show hands holding phones
	•	Show creators smiling at camera
	•	Animate these clips in staggered entry
	•	Mix video frames: 16:9, 9:16, 1:1
	•	Each frame should float slightly (micro motion)

This makes the site feel alive and real.
