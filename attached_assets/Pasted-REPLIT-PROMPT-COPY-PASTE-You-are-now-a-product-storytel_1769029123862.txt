REPLIT PROMPT (COPY–PASTE)

You are now a product storyteller + motion designer, not just a frontend engineer.
Your task is to redesign the hero process animation so it visually communicates the entire Navinta journey, not just recording.

The goal is that a user understands the product without reading a single word.

⸻

CORE IDEA

This section should feel like:

“I sign in → Navinta directs me → I record → AI edits → I export a real video”

It should feel like a mini movie about making a movie.

⸻

REPLACE THE STATIC PHONE WITH A STORY ANIMATION

Instead of showing only a phone recording UI, build a timeline animation inside the phone frame that progresses as the user scrolls.

The phone becomes a stage where scenes change.

⸻

SCENE 1 — ONBOARDING (FIRST VIEW)

When the hero loads:

Inside the phone:
	•	Show 2–3 onboarding cards sliding in
	•	Example cards:
	•	“What do you want people to feel?”
	•	“What’s your brand about?”
	•	“How long should videos be?”
	•	Cards animate in with intention (slow, weighted motion)
	•	A subtle cursor / highlight selects answers
	•	Cards collapse into a single “Plan Created” moment

This immediately tells users: Navinta thinks before you record.

⸻

SCENE 2 — DIRECTOR MODE (SCROLL 1)

As user scrolls slightly:

The phone UI transforms:
	•	The onboarding UI morphs into “Director Mode”
	•	Shot cards slide in from the side
	•	Each shot card shows:
	•	Shot 1: Look at camera
	•	Shot 2: Walk & gesture
	•	Shot 3: Close-up reaction

Add:
	•	subtle timeline at bottom
	•	shots light up one by one
	•	the phone now feels like a control panel, not a camera

⸻

SCENE 3 — RECORDING (SCROLL 2)

As user scrolls more:
	•	Recording UI fades in
	•	Red record dot pulses
	•	Progress bar fills
	•	One shot completes → next shot auto-selects
	•	Small micro-animation: cut happens automatically
	•	Pauses are visually removed (clip tightens)

This visually communicates auto cutting without text.

⸻

SCENE 4 — AI EDITING (SCROLL 3)

Now show the magic:
	•	Captions auto-type on screen
	•	B-roll slides in from edges
	•	Color grade applies with a smooth fade
	•	Music waveform appears
	•	Timeline snaps cleanly into place

This should feel like:

“Editing is happening for me”

⸻

SCENE 5 — EXPORT (FINAL SCROLL)

Final scroll moment:
	•	Phone UI zooms out
	•	Video card floats out of phone
	•	“Exported” badge appears
	•	Card slides into a clean grid (content library)
	•	Everything settles into calm stillness

End with confidence.

⸻

MOTION & STYLE RULES
	•	Use GSAP timelines for each scene
	•	Use ScrollTrigger to control transitions
	•	Animate transforms, opacity, masks, not position jumps
	•	No flashy colors
	•	No gradients
	•	No glow
	•	Use light, shadow, scale, and timing
	•	Each scene should take ~1 scroll length
	•	Motion should feel directed, not playful

⸻

TECH IMPLEMENTATION
	•	Keep one phone frame
	•	Swap content inside using GSAP timelines
	•	Use absolute-positioned layers
	•	Do NOT mount/unmount components
	•	Animate visibility & transforms only
	•	Ensure mobile fallback (static but clean)

⸻

QUALITY BAR

This animation should feel like:
	•	Apple product launch
	•	Stripe checkout flow
	•	Linear onboarding
	•	A24 opening sequence

If it feels like:
	•	demo animation
	•	marketing fluff
	•	template animation

→ redo it.

⸻

FINAL GOAL

A user should finish this animation and think:

“Oh… this thing actually directs me.”

Not:

“This records video.”
